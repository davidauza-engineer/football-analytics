Here is a practice exercise for your new Python lesson.

Assume you're a student tackling a geometry problem: finding the volume of a cube. Remembering your lessons, you recall that the volume of a cube can be calculated using a simple formula: \(\text{Volume} = \text{(side length)}^3\).

Armed with a ruler, you measure one side, finding it to be \(4\) units long. Your task is to apply this formula to find the volume of the cube and print it to the screen by writing a Python program.
